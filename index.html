Mission accomplished.

Mission accomplished is an expression that signals the end of a difficult task.

It can also refer to the end of a military operation, where an invading force declares victory by occupying all territory and eliminating all hostile forces. This is sometimes called "taking over the country." The term was used in World War II to describe Hitler's conquests in Europe.

Some other expressions that mean the same or similar thing include "mission accomplished," "job done," or "mission successful."

What is a goal?

Goals are indicators of what an individual wants to achieve in life. They can be specific or broad, short-term or long-term, and describable in no more than one sentence. One of the most important aspects of goal setting is to keep them SMART: Specific, Measurable, Attainable, Realistic, and Timely.

Goals are usually defined as concrete tasks for us to accomplish. This could be something we want to achieve or a more abstract goal, such as “being less angry.”

We can set goals by writing them down, create a plan for how we want to accomplish the goal and work towards it. Our goals may be short-term (daily) or long-term (2 years).
